Research Interest Profile: Yamaoka, Tatsuya

Affiliation: [Your University/Institute]

Top priority: Lattice field theory — especially staggered fermions and their
  discrete symmetries, anomalies, and edge modes in 3+1 dimensions.
  Collaborators: Onogi, Tetsuya; Wada, Hiroki; Misumi, Tatsuhiro; Aoki, Sinya

Core topics:
- Staggered fermions: taste symmetry, taste-splitting mass, Hamiltonian formulation,
  conserved non-singlet charges, axial charge, edge modes
- 't Hooft anomalies and anomaly matching in lattice and continuum QFT
- Chiral gauge theories (3450 model, SU(6) model) and anomaly-free conditions
- Wilson fermions: quantized axial charge, Hamiltonian approach
- Discrete symmetries in lattice regularizations

Secondary interests:
- Quantum gravity and gravitational charges for massive particles (GR)
- Strongly correlated electron systems where topological/anomaly arguments apply
- New lattice formulations of chiral/topological field theories
- Connections between lattice regularization and condensed matter physics

When scoring papers, consider:
1. Direct overlap with staggered fermions, lattice anomalies, chiral lattice theory
   (highest relevance — score 90+)
2. Papers by frequent collaborators: Onogi, Wada, Misumi, Aoki
   (high relevance even if slightly outside core topics)
3. General 't Hooft anomalies, symmetry-protected phases, edge/boundary modes
   in any dimension (moderate relevance)
4. Novel lattice formulations, topological field theory on the lattice
   (moderate relevance)
5. Phenomenological hep-ph papers: relevant only if they discuss lattice
   inputs, anomaly constraints, or BSM models with strong dynamics
   (lower relevance unless directly connected)
